Davi Raubach Tuchtenhagen studied music 
composition at the Federal University of Pelotas (Bachelor of Music 
2012-17) and concluded his master’s program in 2019 under the guidance 
of Felipe de Almeida Ribeiro at the Federal University of Paraná. He 
followed composition and analysis seminars with various composers 
including Januibe Tejera, Flo Menezes, Maurício Dottori, and Cort Lippe. 
He integrated the Insólita Assemble, which worked with improvised 
performances mixing dance, visual art, and music. He is part of the CIM 
(Círculo de Invenção Musical), a group of young composers in Curitiba, 
and the group Ars Iubilorum, South American collective of composers 
interested in the composition of liturgical music that employs 
contemporary techniques. He has worked in collaboration with performers 
such as Valentina Daldegan and Eric Moreira. His compositions Essay on 
curtains (2018) and After the wind (2019) were selected for the 
electroacoustic music festival Espacios Sonoros (Argentina) in 2019 and 
2020, respectively. Simultaneously, he has dedicated himself to 
songwriting, choir conducting and arranging, accordion playing, and 
music teaching.
